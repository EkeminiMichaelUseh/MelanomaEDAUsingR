Exploratory Data Analysis of
"Survival from malignant melanoma" Dataset (By Ekemini Useh)

Introduction

One type of skin cancer that is mostly brought on by UV rays from the sun is called melanoma. Because human skin is exposed to this dangerous beam daily, further research is important. This report's purposes are to:
- Ascertain the data's summary statistics.
- Analyze the variables using correlation and regression.
- To ascertain whether a patient's sex has an impact on survival time after surgery by a two-sample significance test grouped by patient sex.
- The percentage of malignant melanoma patients who survive after surgery to remove their tumors.

Please click here to see the full documentation on github.

About Data

Data Source / Description

The dataset is the measurements recorded on 205 patients with malignant melanoma whose tumors were removed at the Department of Plastic Surgery, University Hospital of Odense, Denmark, between 1962 and 1977. The data collected were survival time, patient’s status, sex, age, year of surgery, thickness of tumor, and ulcer (if tumor was ulcerated).

Data Cleaning and Preparation

The tool used for data cleaning and preparation is R

Steps Taken


- The less important column was removed.


- The data types of variables status, sex, year, and ulcer were changed from double to factor.


- The values in status, sex, and ulcer were represented in numbers, the labels were changed to the actual inputs for easy data exploration.


- Null values were checked but there was none.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) & Statistical Analysis

Steps Taken


- Summary statistics of the variables.


- Graphical summaries to further explain the statistics visually (Charts, graphs, and plots).


- Regression analysis and correlation computations.


- Two sample significance tests grouped by sex (T-test).

Observations

- The findings gathered from the data show that malignant melanoma is likely to have ulcerations present depending on its tumor thickness.

- Less people are probably going to die from their melanoma because more people live after having their cancer removed.

- When melanoma tumors ulcerate because of their increased thickness, the chance of death from the disease raises.

Recommendations, Conclusion, and Limitations

Recommendations

People need to seek proper medical care and advice at the sight of any skin infection as it could be melanoma. This will hinder the growth and spread of such infection. The possibility of ulceration, if detected early, will decrease and cured without the risk of fatality.

Conclusion

- Melanoma is not fata when detected early.

- The relationship between survival time and tumor thickness is a weak negative correlation. The smaller the tumor thickness, the higher the survival time afetr surgery.

- Time and age had a weak negative correlation. The patient's survival time following melanoma removal decreases with age.

- A weak positive association exists between age and thickness. A larger tumor was possible in the elderly people.
